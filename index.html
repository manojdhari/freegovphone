Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about the free government phone program

What is the free government phone program?

+

The free government phone program includes Lifeline and the Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP), which provide eligible low-income Americans with free smartphones and monthly service including talk, text, and data at no cost.

Who qualifies for a free government phone?

+

You may qualify if you participate in government assistance programs such as SNAP (Food Stamps), Medicaid, SSI, Federal Public Housing Assistance, Veterans Pension, or if your income is at or below 135% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines. The ACP program has expanded eligibility to those with income at or below 200% of Federal Poverty Guidelines.

Is the phone and service really free?

+

Yes. If you qualify, both the smartphone and monthly service are completely free. There are no monthly bills, no contracts, and no hidden fees. This is a legitimate government benefit program designed to ensure all Americans have access to essential communication services.

What documents do I need to apply?

+

You'll need to provide proof of identity (such as a driver's license, state ID, or passport) and proof of eligibility (such as your benefit card or award letter from a qualifying program like SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, etc.). The online application will guide you through the specific documents needed.

How long does the application process take?

+

The online application takes about 5-10 minutes to complete. Once submitted, approval typically takes 1-3 business days. After approval, you'll receive your phone within 5-10 business days.
