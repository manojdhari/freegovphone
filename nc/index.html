About the Free Government Phones Program

The Free Government Phones is a Federal Communications Commission program that helps ensure households can afford the broadband they need for work, school, healthcare and more.

The benefit provides a discount of up to $30 per month toward internet service for eligible households and up to $75 per month for households on qualifying Tribal lands. Eligible households can also receive a one-time discount of up to $100 to purchase a laptop, desktop computer, or tablet from participating providers.

Program Benefits for North Carolina, Residents:

  • Monthly internet service discount up to $30 ($75 on Tribal lands)
  • One-time device discount up to $100
  • No activation fees or equipment rental fees from participating providers
  • Access to affordable internet plans specifically for Free Government Phones participants

Free Government Phones Eligibility Requirements

Income-Based Eligibility

Your household income is at or below 200% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines.

2024 Income Guidelines

Household Size Annual Income
1 person $31,200
2 people $42,400
3 people $53,600
4 people $64,800

Program-Based Eligibility

You or someone in your household participates in one of these programs:

  • SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)
  • Medicaid
  • WIC (Women, Infants, and Children)
  • Federal Public Housing Assistance
  • SSI (Supplemental Security Income)
  • Veterans Pension and Survivors Benefit
  • Lifeline
  • Tribal-specific programs (for Tribal lands)
  • Free and Reduced-Price School Lunch/Breakfast
  • Federal Pell Grant (current award year)

Frequently Asked Questions

The Free Government Phones is a Federal Communications Commission program that helps ensure households can afford the broadband they need for work, school, healthcare and more. It provides discounts on monthly internet service and devices.

Households qualify if their income is at or below 200% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines, or if they participate in certain assistance programs like SNAP, Medicaid, WIC, Federal Public Housing Assistance, SSI, Veterans Pension, Lifeline, or others.

Eligible households can receive up to $30 per month toward internet service ($75 per month for households on qualifying Tribal lands) and a one-time discount of up to $100 for a laptop, desktop computer, or tablet.

You can apply online through the official Free Government Phones application process, by mail, or through participating providers. The application is free and requires proof of identity, address, and eligibility.

Many major internet service providers participate in the Free Government Phones, including Xfinity, Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, and many local providers. You can choose from participating providers in your area after you're approved.
